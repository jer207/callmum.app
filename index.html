Calendars, to-do lists, task managers — they're great at reminding you to buy milk. They're less good at making you feel like calling someone actually matters. When life gets busy, the people who aren't right in front of you have a way of quietly slipping to the back of your mind. Not because you don't care. Just because nothing is pushing back hard enough.

CallMum lets you add the people in your life worth staying in touch with, and set a personal check-in timeframe for each one. When it's been too long, it starts reminding you. Then it reminds you again. The reminders don't make you feel bad, but they don't give up either. Think of it less like a task manager, and more like something quietly insisting you do the thing you already wanted to do.

This app has one job

CallMum doesn't require an account and has no access to the internet — everything it needs to work stays on your phone. No call logs, no contact details, and no personal information of any kind is ever transmitted anywhere. There are no ads, no in-app purchases, and no interest in your data. This app exists to do one small thing well, and nothing else.

Just a mama's boy from Maine.
